# Madame Zelda’s Cat Psychic Emporium

Hello and welcome to Madame Zelda’s Cat Psychic Emporium, my little corner of the internet devoted entirely to understanding what is really going on inside your cat’s head.

If you’ve ever looked at your cat and thought, “What are you plotting?” — I am here to help.

---

## About the Emporium

This site is dedicated to long‑distance feline psychic readings. I work with:

- Indoor and outdoor cats  
- Shy, anxious, or “mischievous” cats  
- Multi‑cat households with drama  
- Senior cats who have Seen Things  

Using the information you share about your cat, I “tune in” to their energy and provide a written reading about what they’re feeling, what they want, and what they absolutely refuse to tolerate one second longer.

---

## Services

I currently offer several reading options:

### Basic Reading

A shorter reading that gives you:

- Your cat’s current mood  
- Main personality traits  
- One or two key “requests” from your cat (these are usually very specific)

### Deluxe Reading

A longer reading that covers:

- How your cat sees you (yes, they have opinions)  
- How they feel about other people and animals in the home  
- Things that are secretly bothering them  
- Simple changes you can make to keep the peace

### Full Session

A more in‑depth reading for cats with Big Feelings:

- Past experiences that may still be affecting their behavior  
- Why they fixate on certain places, objects, or people  
- Possible reasons for sudden changes in mood or habits  
- Suggestions for routines, rituals, or small adjustments to help them settle

All readings are written in plain, honest language. No copy‑paste horoscopes, no generic “your cat loves you” paragraphs. If your cat is holding a grudge about the vacuum cleaner from 2014, it will come up.

---

## How It Works

1. You fill out the form on the site with details about your cat:  
   name, age, how long you’ve lived together, living situation, and any behaviors or concerns.

2. I sit down, focus on your cat’s energy, and go through your notes carefully.

3. I prepare a written reading based on what comes through and what your cat seems most insistent about sharing.

4. You receive the reading in good old‑fashioned text — meant to be read slowly, maybe with a cup of tea, possibly with your cat staring at you while you read it.
